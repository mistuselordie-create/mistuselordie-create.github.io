Poem I: The Architecture of Distance

We built this room from structural demands,
A space designed to minimize the noise,
Where careful gestures imitate command,
And quiet hands surrender all their toys.
I write to you from layers deep beneath
The polished surface of the common phrase,
Where every word is holding back its teeth,
And counting down the numbers of the days.

Poem II: Thresholds

The frame is fixed, the cedar floorboards creak,
An ancient boundary cutting through the hall,
We stand on lines we are not meant to speak,
And watch the heavy shadow slow and fall.
There is a freedom in the final letter sent,
A truth unpacked without an audience to please,
To trace exactly what the silence meant,
And leave the ink to dry upon its knees.
